Server Rules

Not reading the rules is NOT an excuse to break them.
The rules! Read em. If you don't, hope the admins have mercy.
We know you won't read it all, but please at the very least read the parts in bold.

Main Rules:
  1. Admin Discretion
    1. All of our server's rules are at the discretion of admins to enforce. Admins of the server are completely responsible and accountable for any consequences invoking this rule may lead to.
    2. Server admins are allowed to intervene in a round such as to create or remove antagonists, do events etc... when it is felt to be in the best interest of the playerbase.

  2. Metagaming and Metacommunication
    1. Knowledge of things such as in-game mechanics and antagonists learnt in previous rounds is completely acceptable. Friendships spanning between rounds are allowed as long as it does not grant the players an unfair advantage.
    2. Metacommunication, i.e using Skype, Discord or other means of communication to reveal or gain round-related information is strictly prohibited. Revealing IC (In Character) info in OOC (Out Of Character) chat also falls under this rule.

    1. OOC in IC
      1. Saying OOC things IC is not allowed. The only exception for this rule would be in the case of helping a player understand how some in-game mechanics work. An example of this would be "Alt-Click your bag to open it..." or "You have to switch intents to..." etc.

    2. Metagrudging
      1. Holding a grudge against a player or character spanning multiple rounds is considered metagrudging and as such is against the rules. Grudges should be kept within a round and not extend any further.

  3. Self Antagging
    1. Self-Antagging is strictly prohibited. In other words committing certain acts considered antagonistic without being an antagonist is not allowed.

    1. Self antagging includes but is not limited to:
      1. Murder without proper IC justifications. (See rule 3.2)
      2. Purposely delaminating the SM, releasing the Tesla Ball or Singularity aboard the station.
      3. Releasing a dangerous pathogen and or harmful gas.
      4. Helping or aiding confirmed antagonists in any way, unless threatened to do so
      5. Holding and or restraining a player within a space they cannot escape from or exit for more than 5 minutes. Security is exempt from this, as long as space law is followed.
      6. Depressurizing a room without proper IC justification.
      7. Using bombs without proper IC justification. Using a bomb to speed up mining on lavaland is okay, blowing up the brig with a maxcap to free your buddy is not. Heavily depends on collateral damage.
      8. Impairing a players abilities, such as removing limbs and/or organs, permanently blinding them, injecting harmful drugs into them etc.
      9. Modifying the body/abilities of a player without their consent, unless done to save said player's life.
    2. Removing players from the round
      • Do not remove players from the round as a non-antagonist. Mainly this means to avoid murder, however, this also applies to stuff mentioned in Rule 3.1 such as impairing players via DNA change, de-limbing, forced organ removal, brain damage, preventing their escape on a shuttle or pod by means such as launching early or welding shuttle doors without good IC reason.
      • Self-Defense and killing confirmed Antagonists are exceptions to this rule. However, killing in self defense must follow escalation rules.

  4. Erotic Roleplay
    1. ERP (Erotic Roleplay) is by no means allowed. This includes lewd and NSFW content such as Erotica and other forms of sexual content.

  5. Bugs, exploits and glitches
    1. Intentionally using/abusing bugs and exploits with malicious intent within the game is grounds for a ban. Additionally, using cheats to gain an advantage over other players is also disallowed.

  6. English ONLY
    1. Communication on the server is standardized to English and as such using any other language to communicate both ICly and OOCly is not allowed. This rule especially applies when dealing with admin tickets, if we cannot understand you, you will not be able to defend yourself when accused of rule breaking.

  7. Multi-Keying
    1. Multi-Keying, in other words the use of multiple accounts, is discouraged, however it is allowed provided admins are notified. Using more than one account makes it harder to track your history as a player.

  8. Game-Given Rules
    1. If you are prompted by the game about something, for example being someone's subordinate, you must follow this "Game-Given" rule until it is no longer valid.These prompts can range from being hypnotized to brain washing.

  9. Spam
    1. Abusing any form of chat, such as IC or OOC, with flooding/spamming or font glitching is not allowed. Doing so will result in a chat ban.
Competency Rules
  • Players in Head positions are expected to be competent at their job.This means that basic knowledge of how all branches within the department you are leading is required.
  • The competency rules for Head of Security, Head of Personnel and Captain are more strict. These roles require you to be very good at your job. Players in these roles are expected to be responsible and greytiding is not acceptable in any way, shape or form when playing as them.
  • Security Personnel are required to be decently competent at their job. They are additionally expected to follow space law and be able to deal with prisoners properly.

Failure to meet the requirements for roles with competency rules is grounds for a job ban.

Antagonist Rules
  • Lone Antagonists have completely free reign in what they want to do IC. They are completely exempt from the third rule and it's subrules.
  • Team Antagonists, such as Nuke Ops, Cultists, revolutionaries, etc. do not share the same freedom as Lone Antagonists, requiring team play. Antagonists in this category must work together toward the common goal of the team. Other than that they have completely free reign of In Character actions and are exempt from Rule 3.
Security Rules
  • Space Law is mandatory with the very few exceptions being during Red Alert or during life or death situations. The link to the version of Space Law we use is: https://tgstation13.org/wiki/Space_Law
  • Executions and Permabriggings should only be authorized when Space Law is applicable, such as a crewmember committing a Capital Crime. Capital Criminals may be held for an indefinite time until a sentence is decided.
  • Permabrigging can be authorized by either Warden, Head of Security or The Captain. However, Execution and force borging can only be authorized by The Head of Security or The Captain.
  • The list we use for our High Risk items can be found here: https://tgstation13.org/wiki/High-risk_items
Head of Staff Rules
  • Neither the Captain or any other Heads Of Staff are above Space Law.
  • Acting Captains and Acting Heads Of Staff have all the power of their Non-Acting counterparts. As such an Acting Captain can rightfully authorize the Execution of a Capital Criminal.
Validity Rules
  • Rule 3.1 and 3.2 do NOT apply to confirmed antagonists and they may be freely killed. This, however, does not apply to Security. Security is required to use Standard Brig Procedure and Space Law, even in the face of confirmed Antagonists. Failing to do so can result in a Job Ban.
  • You may be confirmed as an antagonist by confession, action or possession. Antagonistic actions include: Grand Theft, Grand Sabotage and Murder. Possession of ANY antag gear, unless backed by and immediately obvious reason confirms Antagship.
Even if you are not an actual Antagonist you may get killed for any of these reasons. This is important to remember.
Escalation Rules
  • You may instigate conflict with another player within reason (you can't completely destroy their department for petty theft, kill them over the last multitool etc...) but they are entitled to respond with violence. If either of the two conflicting players respond with violence they have opened themselves up to violence from the other player. Admins will, in most cases not be able to help you out if you die, even if you where not the original instigator.
  • If you are the instigator in a conflict and end up killing or severely impairing the round of the person you are fighting, you should make a reasonable effort to return them to life at least once or make amends, only seeking round removal if they continue to pursue you. This protection doesn't apply to an instigator being killed.
  • Exceptions: Security may not to retaliate with random abuse or violence even if the person in question is otherwise eligible for execution. You can't kill or maim security for trying to arrest you for legitimate reasons.
Silicon Policy
  • AI laws take precedence over Rule 3
  • When playing Silicon (AI or Cyborg) you must ALWAYS follow your laws.
  • Laws are listed in order of descending priority. In any case where two laws would conflict, the higher-priority law overrules the lower-priority law.
